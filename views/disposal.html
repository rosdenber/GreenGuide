Proper Disposal Instructions

Step-by-step instructions on how to dispose of common items properly

Household Items

Proper disposal methods for common household waste items.

Batteries

  1. Do not throw in regular trash
  2. Take to designated battery recycling centers
  3. Many retailers accept used batteries
  4. Tape terminals to prevent short circuits
Note: Rechargeable batteries require special handling. Contact local recycling centers for specific instructions.

Light Bulbs

  1. LED bulbs: Can be recycled at hardware stores
  2. Incandescent bulbs: Wrap in paper and dispose in residual waste
  3. Fluorescent bulbs: Take to hazardous waste facilities
  4. Handle carefully to avoid breakage
Note: Broken fluorescent bulbs contain mercury and require special disposal procedures.

Aerosol Cans

  1. Use up all contents completely
  2. Remove plastic cap and dispose separately
  3. Place empty can in recycling bin
  4. If partially full, take to hazardous waste facility

Electronics

Safe disposal methods for electronic devices to prevent environmental contamination.

Mobile Phones

  1. Backup and erase all personal data
  2. Remove SIM and memory cards
  3. Find certified e-waste recyclers
  4. Many manufacturers offer take-back programs
Note: Mobile phones contain valuable materials that can be recovered through proper recycling.

Computers

  1. Backup important data to cloud or external storage
  2. Use factory reset to wipe hard drive
  3. Donate if still functional
  4. Otherwise, take to certified e-waste facilities

Hazardous Waste

Special disposal instructions for items that can harm human health or the environment.

Paint and Solvents

  1. Do not pour down drains or on the ground
  2. Take to hazardous waste collection events
  3. Latex paint: Dry out and dispose in residual waste
  4. Oil-based paint: Always take to hazardous waste facilities

Medications

  1. Do not flush down the toilet
  2. Remove personal information from labels
  3. Take to pharmacy medication take-back programs
  4. Mix with undesirable substances before disposal

Special Items

Disposal instructions for items that require special handling or have unique requirements.

Textiles

  1. Donate wearable items to charity
  2. Check for textile recycling programs
  3. Cut up damaged items to prevent reuse
  4. Place in designated textile recycling bins

Tires

  1. Do not illegally dump tires
  2. Take to tire retailers or recycling centers
  3. Some auto shops offer tire disposal services
  4. Check for local tire recycling programs

Educate. Segregate. Sustain.
