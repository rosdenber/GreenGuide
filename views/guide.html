Waste Segregation Guides

Learn how to properly sort your waste into the right categories

Biodegradable Waste

Biodegradable waste consists of organic materials from households that can decompose naturally and become compost.

Common Items:

  • Food scraps (fruits, vegetables, bread)
  • Coffee grounds and tea bags
  • Eggshells and nutshells
  • Used paper towels and napkins (unbleached)
  • Wood chips and sawdust (untreated)

Food Scraps

Coffee Grounds

Paper Towels

What to Do

  • Use a dedicated compost bin
  • Chop larger items into smaller pieces
  • Mix "greens" (food scraps) with "browns" (dry leaves)
  • Turn the compost regularly for aeration

What Not to Do

  • Don't include meat or dairy products
  • Don't add pet waste or diapers
  • Don't include diseased plants
  • Don't add oils or fats

Non-Biodegradable Waste

Non-biodegradable waste consists of materials that do not decompose naturally and persist in the environment for long periods.

Common Items:

  • Ceramics and porcelain
  • Rubber products (tires, gloves)
  • Composite materials (mixed plastics)
  • Tetra Pak containers
  • Polystyrene (Styrofoam)

Ceramics

Rubber Products

Composite Packaging

What to Do

  • Check if specialized recycling programs exist
  • Dispose at designated collection points
  • Consider reusing items when possible
  • Follow local waste management guidelines

What Not to Do

  • Don't throw in regular trash bins without checking
  • Don't burn these materials
  • Don't mix with recyclable materials
  • Don't dispose of in nature

Recyclable Waste

Recyclable waste consists of materials that can be processed and reused to make new products.

Common Items:

  • Paper products (newspapers, magazines, cardboard)
  • Plastic bottles and containers (check for recycling symbols)
  • Glass bottles and jars
  • Metal cans (aluminum, steel)
  • Clean aluminum foil

Newspaper

Glass Bottles

Plastic Bottles

What to Do

  • Rinse containers before recycling
  • Remove caps and lids (unless specified otherwise)
  • Flatten cardboard boxes to save space
  • Check local guidelines for specific materials

What Not to Do

  • Don't recycle plastic bags (take to grocery stores)
  • Don't include greasy pizza boxes
  • Don't recycle broken glass or ceramics
  • Don't mix different materials together

Green Waste

Green waste consists of organic materials from gardens and parks that can be composted or mulched.

Common Items:

  • Grass clippings
  • Leaves and tree prunings
  • Weeds and plant trimmings
  • Small branches (under 10cm diameter)
  • Flowers and plants

Leaves

Grass Clippings

Tree Prunings

What to Do

  • Compost in a dedicated bin or pile
  • Use as mulch around plants
  • Dry leaves before composting
  • Chop larger materials into smaller pieces

What Not to Do

  • Don't include diseased plants
  • Don't add weeds with seeds
  • Don't include pet waste
  • Don't compost treated wood or plants

Hazardous Waste

Hazardous waste consists of materials that can be harmful to human health or the environment and require special handling.

Common Items:

  • Batteries (especially rechargeable and button batteries)
  • Paints, solvents, and chemicals
  • Motor oil and automotive fluids
  • Fluorescent light bulbs and LEDs
  • Electronic waste (phones, computers, etc.)

Batteries

Paint & Solvents

Electronics

What to Do

  • Take to designated hazardous waste collection centers
  • Store in original containers with labels intact
  • Keep away from children and pets
  • Participate in community collection events

What Not to Do

  • Don't throw in regular trash or recycling bins
  • Don't mix different chemicals together
  • Don't pour down drains or on the ground
  • Don't store for extended periods without proper containment
