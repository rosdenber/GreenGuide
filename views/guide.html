Waste Segregation Guides

Learn how to properly sort your waste into the right categories

Recyclable Waste

Recyclable waste consists of materials that can be processed and reused to make new products.

Common Items:

  • Paper products (newspapers, magazines, cardboard)
  • Plastic bottles and containers (check for recycling symbols)
  • Glass bottles and jars
  • Metal cans (aluminum, steel)
  • Clean aluminum foil

Glass Bottles

Plastic Bottles

What to Do

  • Rinse containers before recycling
  • Remove caps and lids (unless specified otherwise)
  • Flatten cardboard boxes to save space
  • Check local guidelines for specific materials

What Not to Do

  • Don't recycle plastic bags (take to grocery stores)
  • Don't include greasy pizza boxes
  • Don't recycle broken glass or ceramics
  • Don't mix different materials together

Biodegradable Waste

Biodegradable waste consists of organic materials that can decompose naturally and become compost.

Common Items:

  • Food scraps (fruits, vegetables, bread)
  • Coffee grounds and tea bags
  • Eggshells and nutshells
  • Garden waste (leaves, grass clippings)
  • Paper towels and napkins (unbleached)

Fruit Scraps

Garden Waste

Coffee Grounds

What to Do

  • Use a dedicated compost bin
  • Chop larger items into smaller pieces
  • Mix "greens" (food scraps) with "browns" (dry leaves)
  • Turn the compost regularly for aeration

What Not to Do

  • Don't include meat or dairy products
  • Don't add pet waste or diapers
  • Don't include diseased plants
  • Don't add oils or fats

General Waste

General waste consists of materials that cannot be recycled or composted and must go to landfill.

Common Items:

  • Plastic wrap and Styrofoam
  • Used tissues and paper towels
  • Cigarette butts
  • Broken ceramics and light bulbs
  • Disposable diapers

Plastic Wrap

Used Tissues

Light Bulbs

What to Do

  • Ensure items are completely dry
  • Place in designated general waste bin
  • Minimize waste by choosing reusable alternatives
  • Check if hazardous items have special disposal locations

What Not to Do

  • Don't burn waste in open areas
  • Don't mix with recyclables or compost
  • Don't include electronic waste
  • Don't dispose of hazardous materials here
